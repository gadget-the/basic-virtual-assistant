This idea is in regards to chatbots and me trying to figure out how to parse through chat logs in order to supply one with trainning data(sort of built around a hypothetical)
It also employs what I call a "Statement-Response" type of system, in which everything said within a conversation is can be a statement or a response(or both). If I said something, that would be a statement, if you said something back to me(in response) that would be considered a response
I pair theses statements and responses together and store them
The goal is to take a chat log(currently in the form of a text file) and convert it into statement-response pairs and add them to a dictionary/json file
after saving many responses, one would probably want to order them by most fitting/most common in order to be able to supply the most likely response to a statement

This could be used for a sort of "Crowdsourcing" of data for a chatbot(similar to what they do over at Cleverbot)


I made a respond function that outputs a random corresponding response from the json file
At first it had to match the exact statement(key) but, I changed it so that it finds the key that shares the most letters(if there is one that shares any letters) and returns a random corresponding response
this seems to work somewhat well
